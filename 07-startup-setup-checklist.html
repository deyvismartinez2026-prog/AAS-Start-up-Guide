Formation · privacy · operating setup

Make AAS real, without making your home address the company.

This checklist fills the missing startup layer: Florida LLC setup, digital office/mail handling, EIN, banking, bookkeeping, templates, social presence, SAM.gov, VetCert, and the compliance calendar that keeps the company clean.

First principle.

Set the address/privacy plan before filing anything. Public records are hard to un-ring once a home address, personal email, or personal phone gets indexed.

Privacy reality check

Privacy means reducing exposure, not hiding from required systems.

Do this
  • Use a registered agent instead of yourself as public service-of-process contact.
  • Use a legitimate commercial/digital office or CMRA mailbox for business mail handling.
  • Use a business email and VoIP number for public records and public-facing accounts.
  • Keep a private records folder for personal identity, VA, bank, IRS, and certification documents.
Do not do this
  • Do not put your home address on Sunbiz, website footer, proposals, social profiles, or email signatures unless there is no alternative.
  • Do not use fake addresses. Banks, SAM.gov, IRS, payment processors, and certification programs may require real verification.
  • Do not let third-party “free LLC” services upsell you into unnecessary filings or use their address without understanding mail/control rules.
  • Do not file before an attorney/CPA/registered agent has reviewed the operating agreement/control structure for SDVOSB readiness.
Florida-specific notes

Sunbiz indicates a principal office address requires a street address; a P.O. Box is not acceptable for that field. Sunbiz also warns that email addresses can be public records. Keep personal contact details out of public filings wherever possible.

Vendor stack

Services to choose deliberately.

Registered agent

Receives legal/service-of-process mail. Confirm renewal cost, privacy posture, scan notifications, and how they handle urgent documents.

Digital office / mailbox

Handles routine mail scanning/forwarding. Confirm CMRA terms, address use cases, mail SLAs, and whether bank/SAM verification support is available.

Attorney + CPA

Attorney for operating agreement/control language; CPA for tax classification, bookkeeping, owner draws, sales-tax and local-license questions.

Banking

Business checking, tax reserve savings, future business credit card, and clean separation between personal and company funds.

Insurance

General liability, professional/E&O, cyber, and workers comp if applicable. Save COIs and renewal dates.

Operating tools

Email, calendar booking, cloud drive, e-signature, CRM, invoicing, form capture, password manager, backup/export routine.

Working templates

Starter files created for you.

These are practical working templates, not legal documents. Use them to run the setup and then have attorney/CPA review anything contractual or tax-sensitive.
