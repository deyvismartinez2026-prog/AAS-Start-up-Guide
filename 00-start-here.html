How the documents connect

Read once, then run the loop.

  1. Read the Business Plan (01) end to end once — it is the why behind every step elsewhere. Skim-stop on Section 3 (offers), Section 7 (go-to-market), Section 10 (numbers).
  2. Open Startup Setup (07) before filing anything — it is the privacy/address/EIN/bank/template checklist that keeps your home address and personal contact details out of public-facing startup plumbing.
  3. Open the Launch Guide (03) and put Phase 0 on this week's calendar. It sequences legal and federal work so nothing lands out of order.
  4. Run the 90-Day Action Plan (06) as the daily/weekly driver. It now interleaves startup setup, privacy, federal registration, templates, social presence, and revenue work.
  5. Keep the workbook (05) honest monthly — real numbers replace planning numbers; the scenario tabs tell you which plan you are actually on.
  6. Send documents 02 and 04 outward — the executive summary to advisors and lenders, the capability statement to buyers and primes, both kept current as placeholders fill.

The one thing not to skip

The operating agreement's control language (Launch Guide step 1.2) is where SDVOSB certifications quietly die. It is a few hundred dollars of attorney time against the asset that opens a $28.6B market. Do it properly, once.

Maintaining the package

  • Edit: open the matching HTML file in the Source folder, change text, save.
  • Rebuild: run ./build.sh in that folder (or ./build.sh 04 for one document). PDFs regenerate into the package folder.
  • Version: bump the v1.0 marks when documents change materially; date every externally shared copy.
  • Workbook: edit blue cells only; black and green cells are formulas. Save copies before big scenario experiments.
Quarterly ritual

Each quarter: actuals into the workbook, scoreboard review from the 90-day plan, business-plan assumptions revised, and a fresh 90-day plan written. The package stays alive or it stops being true.

How this package was built

Strategy and pricing derive from the founder's own doctrine (BUSINESS_GAME_PLAN.md, PROJECT.md, BRAND.md) and the live site at aas-fable.vercel.app. Regulatory facts — certification rules, thresholds, timelines, program terms — were verified against official and industry sources as of June 2026; each major document carries its own source list and the business plan's Appendix C is the master list. Financial figures are founder planning assumptions, marked as sample placeholders throughout.
