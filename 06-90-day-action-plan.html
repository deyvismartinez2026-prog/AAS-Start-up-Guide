90-day action plan — Operating rules
How this plan works

Three lanes, run in parallel, reviewed weekly.

Lane A — Revenue

Workshops, audits, and the first pilot. The funnel sequence from the business plan: educate → qualify → diagnose → decide. This lane pays for everything.

Lane B — Federal & admin

The Launch Guide phases 0–4 on a calendar: formation, registrations, certification, contract readiness. Mostly waiting wrapped around short bursts of paperwork.

Lane C — Platform & content

Teardown pieces, workshop outlines, and platform delivery discipline. Every artifact becomes funnel fuel or proof.

The weekly operating hour

Every Friday, thirty to sixty minutes against the scoreboard: what closed, what moved, what stalled, and the three most important actions for next week — one per lane. The discipline is the strategy.

Day 1–30 goal · make the company real

Privacy-first Florida LLC setup plan · registered agent + digital office/mail handling selected · LLC filed after address plan is settled · operating agreement drafted · EIN issued · bank account and tax reserve live · bookkeeping/receipt capture running · basic licenses/insurance checked · website lead capture wired.

Day 31–60 goal · operate and register

Working templates live (proposal, SOW, invoice, W-9 packet, discovery, kickoff, follow-up) · CRM/pipeline live · social profiles and branded email signature live · SAM submitted/active · DSBS profile drafted · VetCert packet staged and submitted · first workshops delivered.

Day 61–90 goal · convert proof and stay compliant

1–2 Foundation audits closed · VetCert approval target · capability statement and website updated with UEI/CAGE/certification status · supplier portals started · sources-sought responses filed · compliance calendar locked for annual report, SAM renewal, insurance, taxes, registered agent, mailbox, and records.

Ground rule

If a week forces a choice between lanes, Lane A wins. Cash and proof make every other lane easier; nothing in Lane B or C is harmed by a one-week slip except certification document deadlines — never miss those.

90-day action plan — Startup setup map
Basic startup steps — not optional, now on the plan

Formation, privacy, banking, templates, social, and federal readiness.

1 · Privacy-first address plan

Registered agent, legitimate digital office/CMRA mailbox, public business email, business phone, mail scanning/forwarding owner, no home address on public-facing materials.

2 · Florida LLC

Sunbiz name check, Articles of Organization filed only after address plan is set, operating agreement drafted, state document number saved, annual report due May 1 calendared.

3 · EIN + bank

Apply for EIN free directly through IRS after LLC approval; open business checking and tax reserve; fund account; connect bookkeeping and receipt capture.

4 · Operating stack

Business email, calendar booking, cloud drive, password manager, e-signature, CRM/pipeline, invoice/payment workflow, and website form that captures real leads.

5 · Working templates

Proposal, SOW, service terms placeholder, invoice, W-9 packet, discovery questionnaire, kickoff agenda, meeting notes, follow-up sequence, capability statement.

6 · Public presence

LinkedIn company page, founder profile, social handles, branded email signature, basic content calendar, and consistent language across website, socials, proposals, and emails.

7 · SAM + SDVOSB

Login.gov, SAM.gov registration, UEI/CAGE, DSBS profile, NAICS/PSC choices, VetCert packet, MySBA Certifications application, APEX/SCORE/VBOC cadence.

8 · Compliance calendar

Florida annual report, SAM renewal, insurance renewal, tax deadlines, registered agent/mailbox renewals, password/access audit, quarterly records review.

Privacy guardrail: privacy means reducing public exposure, not hiding from required systems. Banks, IRS, SAM.gov, payment processors, and certification bodies can still require truthful owner/responsible-party information. Use attorney/CPA review for the operating agreement, tax posture, and SDVOSB control language.

Floor = the plan survives. Target = the base case is on track. Below floor on two consecutive reviews → cut scope, not corners: fewer lanes, same discipline.

If revenue runs ahead

Do not add offers. Raise the audit price test ($30K), tighten qualification, and bank the surplus — the analyst hire trigger (two concurrent audits) arrives sooner than you think.

If revenue runs behind

Double Lane A workshops, halve content cadence, and ask VBOC/APEX to pressure-test the offer and the list. The fixed cost base (≈$5K/mo) buys patience; use it on the funnel, not on new ideas.

Build reputation before extraction. The quarter is won in the weekly hour.
