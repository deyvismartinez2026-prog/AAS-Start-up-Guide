SDVOSB Launch Guide — How to use
Before you start

Order matters. That is the whole point.

Most veteran founders lose months to sequence mistakes: applying for certification before the operating agreement supports it, registering in SAM before the bank account exists, or waiting for certification before selling anything. This guide exists so every step lands once, in the right order.

The golden chain

LLC formed → operating agreement with control language → EIN → business bank account → SAM.gov registration → SBA VetCert certification → capability statement with UEI/CAGE → buyers. Each link needs the one before it. Everything else in this guide runs in parallel around that chain.

Three rules while you work the guide

  1. Never pay for what the government gives free. SAM registration, UEI, CAGE, and SDVOSB certification cost $0. Anyone charging "filing assistance" fees for them is selling you your own paperwork (see Scams, page 27).

  3. Document as you go. Every approval letter, screenshot, and confirmation number goes in one folder. Future-you (and the SBA examiner) will thank you.

0.1

Pull your VA paperwork

Cost $0 Time 30–60 min Depends on nothing — start here

Log in at va.gov → "Records" → download your Benefit Summary Letter (also called the VA award/rating letter — it must show a service-connected disability rating; any rating qualifies, including 0%). Locate your DD-214 (member-4 copy preferred); if missing, request it free via milConnect or the National Archives (archives.gov/veterans). Save both as PDFs in your launch folder.

Done whenBenefit Summary Letter and DD-214 are saved as clean PDFs you can upload in under a minute.
0.2

Enroll the free advisor bench

Cost $0 Time 2–3 hrs Depends on nothing — parallel

Three appointments, all free, all reusable for years: (1) VBOC — find your regional Veterans Business Outreach Center (sba.gov → local assistance → VBOC; 31 centers nationwide) and book an intake; ask about the next Boots to Business Reboot class. (2) APEX Accelerator — your government-contracting counselor (apexaccelerators.us); they will later run bid-match searches and review proposals. (3) SCORE — a standing monthly mentor for operating accountability.

Done whenVBOC intake booked, APEX enrollment submitted, SCORE mentor request sent.
0.3

Confirm the entity plan with a CPA

Cost $0–$300 Time 1 hr Depends on 0.2 (VBOC can refer)

Default plan: single-member LLC in your home state, taxed as sole proprietorship now, S-corp election revisited with the CPA once net income stabilizes (often worthwhile around $60–$80K+). Do not form a C-corp for this business model, and do not form in Delaware/Wyoming because a video said so — federal contracting does not care, and foreign-LLC registration in your home state doubles fees. One hour with a CPA now prevents a re-formation later.

Done whenEntity type, state, and tax posture are confirmed in writing (even an email).

Optional but valuable — open a VR&E conversation (Chapter 31)

Cost $0 Time 1–2 hrs to start Depends on 0.1

If your service-connected disability creates an employment barrier, the VR&E Self-Employment track can fund business-plan development, startup supplies, equipment, and training — plus a subsistence allowance while enrolled. Phase 0 exit criteria

VA documents in hand · advisor bench enrolled · entity plan confirmed · name and domain locked · VR&E decision made. You have spent almost nothing and removed the four most common early blockers.

Veteran-status bonus — check before paying state fees

Several states waive formation costs for veteran-owned companies. Texas, for example, waives the $300 LLC filing fee and the franchise tax for the first five years for 100% veteran-owned businesses (made permanent in 2025; requires a Texas Veterans Commission verification letter). Search "[your state] veteran LLC fee waiver" and your state veterans commission before filing — five minutes that can save real money.

Phase 1 — Form the company
1

Form the company

Week 1–3 · The legal foundation everything else cites
1.1

File the LLC

Cost $0–$500 (state fee) Time 1 hr / approval often same-day to 2 wks Depends on 0.3, 0.4

File Articles of Organization with your state's Secretary of State — online, directly, yourself. You do not need LegalZoom-style services for a single-member LLC; the state form takes under an hour. List yourself as the organizer and sole member. Use a real street address (SAM.gov dislikes PO boxes). Designate a registered agent: yourself if you keep reliable business hours at that address, or a commercial agent (~$100–$200/yr) for privacy.

Done whenStamped/approved Articles of Organization PDF is in your launch folder.
1.2

Execute an operating agreement that protects your certification

Cost $500–$1,500 (attorney) Time 2–4 hrs Depends on 1.1

This is the document the SBA examiner reads line by line. A generic template can sink the application even at 100% ownership. Have a small-business attorney draft or review it specifically for 13 CFR Part 128 compliance.

Control language the examiner looks for

You as sole managing member holding the highest officer title · ownership direct and unconditional (no conditions, no trusts that break "direct," no unexercised options held by others) · no provision requiring anyone else's consent for ordinary or strategic decisions · no transfer restrictions, rights of first refusal, or forced-sale clauses that condition your ownership · profits and losses to you in proportion to ownership · your full-time devotion to the company stated. If you later add partners or investors, re-screen every clause against Part 128 before signing.

Done whenSigned operating agreement is on file and the attorney has confirmed Part 128 compatibility in writing.

Open the business bank account and bookkeeping

Cost $0–$25/mo Time 1–2 hrs Depends on 1.1, 1.2, 1.3

Bring Articles, operating agreement, and EIN letter. Open business checking plus a savings sub-account for the 25% tax set-aside (mirrors the financial model). Connect bookkeeping software from day one — clean books become the financial statements lenders, the VR&E counselor, and eventually contracting officers ask for. Federal registrations

Week 3–5 · Free, and the gateway to everything federal
2.1

Create your Login.gov account

Cost $0 Time 15 min Depends on nothing

One Login.gov identity now opens SAM.gov, the SBA certification portal, and most federal systems. Use the business email from step 0.4 and enroll two authentication methods (authenticator app + backup codes stored offline).

Done whenLogin.gov works with two MFA methods registered.
2.2

Register the entity in SAM.gov

Cost $0 — always Time 1–3 hrs entry / 7–10 business days typical (new entities up to 3–4 wks) Depends on 1.1–1.4

At sam.gov → Workspace → Entities → Register Entity, choose "All Awards." The flow: (1) entity validation — legal name, street address, and start date are checked against state records (mismatches cause most delays: enter everything exactly as the Articles read); your UEI is assigned automatically; (2) core data — EIN, banking for EFT (from step 1.4); (3) NAICS — enter the codes from the appendix table, 541512 primary; (4) size metrics — receipts and employees (zeros are fine for a new firm); (5) Representations & Certifications — answer carefully; your SDVOSB status reps become live once VetCert issues; (6) points of contact. Your CAGE code is assigned automatically by DLA after validation. Calendar the annual renewal immediately — registration lapses kill eligibility mid-pursuit.

Done whenSAM status shows Active; UEI and CAGE recorded in the launch folder and dropped into the capability statement.
Scam interception — starts the day you register

Your SAM record is public, and "registration help" mills harvest it. Expect calls and emails offering to "complete," "renew," or "expedite" SAM for $400–$900. SAM.gov is free, renewal is free, and no third party is required — official mail comes from .gov addresses only. Forward the rest to spam.

Phase 3 — SDVOSB certification
3.2

Apply at veterans.certify.sba.gov

Cost $0 — certification is free Time 1–2 hrs / ~12 days avg decision (plan for 30) Depends on 3.1

Sign in with Login.gov at the SBA's Veteran Small Business Certification portal, start the SDVOSB application, answer the ownership/control questionnaire exactly as your documents read, and upload the staged set. The SBA verifies veteran and disability status directly with VA data — which is why the Benefit Summary Letter matters more than any paid "certification consultant." What examiners probe hardest: control (does anyone else co-sign decisions?), outside employment (can you actually run this full-time?), license dependencies (does the business depend on someone else's professional license? — not applicable to AAS), and operating-agreement conditions on ownership.

Done whenApplication shows Submitted, then Approved; certification letter PDF saved.
3.3

After approval — make the status work

Cost $0 Time 1 hr Depends on 3.2

Calendar recertification at the 3-year mark (start 90 days early). Update: SAM reps & certs, the DSBS profile, the capability statement chip from "pending" to certified, the website footer, and your email signature. Material changes (new owners, new officers, outside employment) must be reported — re-screen against Part 128 before any such change. If denied: read the reasons, fix the document or structure issue, and reapply; your APEX counselor reviews the package free.

Done whenCertification shows in the VetCert registry and your DSBS profile displays SDVOSB status.
Phase 3 exit criteria

Certified SDVOSB, three-year term, recertification calendared. Set-asides, sole source up to $5M, VA Vets First priority, and prime subcontracting goals are now legally addressable.

Never pay to be certified

Certification consultants charging $2,000–$5,000 to "handle VetCert" are filling in the same free form. The only paid professional who belongs in this phase is your attorney on the operating agreement — and that work happened in Phase 1.

3.5

Stack the state & corporate certifications

After federal certification · Mostly free · Compounds discoverability

Federal certification is the anchor; these registrations multiply where it gets seen. Do them in one focused week once the SBA letter lands.

3.5a

State veteran/disabled-veteran business programs

Cost usually $0Time 1–3 hrsDepends on 3.2

Most states run veteran-business certification or registry programs with state-contract preferences or goals (California's DVBE program carries a 3% participation goal; New York's SDVOB program 6%; Texas runs HUB and veteran registries — names and rules vary). Many accept the SBA VetCert letter as primary evidence. Register in your home state first, then in any state where target clients sit.

Done whenHome-state veteran certification or registry listing is issued.
3.5b

Corporate supplier-diversity portals

Cost $0Time 2–4 hrs totalDepends on 3.2

Large corporations and defense primes track veteran-owned spend and search their own supplier portals first. Register AAS (with the SBA letter) in the supplier-diversity systems of 5–10 target primes and enterprises — defense integrators for the federal engine, large regional firms for the commercial one. This is also how subcontracting conversations start in Phase 5.

Done whenProfiles live in at least five target portals; confirmation emails filed.
Prioritization rule

Do not chase every certification that exists. The test: does a real buyer you target check this box? Federal SDVOSB — always. Home state — yes. A third state's registry — only when a deal points there.

Build your government rate card

Cost $0–$300 (CPA hour)Time 2–3 hrsDepends on 0.3

Federal buyers think in loaded labor rates even when you sell fixed-fee. Build a simple defensible card: base salary ÷ 2,080 hours × a loaded multiplier (≈2.0–2.4 covering taxes, benefits, overhead, G&A, margin). Sample method (placeholders — set your own with the CPA):

Labor categoryBase salary×2.2 loaded /hr
AI solutions architect (founder)$150,000≈ $159
Automation engineer$110,000≈ $116
AI adoption trainer$90,000≈ $95

Map fixed-fee offers onto these rates so any government price analyst can reconstruct your math — the same conservative-math posture the audit sells.

4.3

Manufacture past performance deliberately

Cost $0Time ongoing habitDepends on first commercial wins

Federal evaluators accept commercial past performance — most new SDVOSBs just fail to document it. From engagement one: capture scope, dollar value, period, measurable outcome (the audit readout produces this automatically), and a named reference who agreed in writing to take a call. Three documented engagements with metrics beat ten vague logos. Subcontract performance under a prime counts too, and your CPARS record starts with the first federal work.

Done whenA past-performance sheet with 3+ documented references exists and stays current.
4.4

Stand up your opportunity radar

Cost $0Time 2 hrs setup, 30 min/wkDepends on 2.2

(1) SAM.gov saved searches — your NAICS codes × set-aside types (SDVOSB set-aside, sole source) × keywords ("artificial intelligence," "automation," "process improvement," "training"); daily email alerts on. Include sources sought and presolicitation notice types — that is where shaping happens. (2) USAspending.gov — search who currently wins SDVOSB awards in your NAICS: those primes are teaming targets and those agencies are warm buyers. (3) Agency forecasts — every agency OSDBU publishes a procurement forecast; start with VA's. (4) APEX bid-match — your counselor sets up a daily matched-opportunity feed, free.

Done whenDaily alerts arrive; the Federal Pipeline tab in the workbook has its first five real entries.
The radar is for relationships, not just bids

4.6

Master the sources-sought response — the highest-leverage page in govcon

Cost $0Time 2–4 hrs eachDepends on 3.2, 4.1

A sources-sought notice is market research before a solicitation. When certified SDVOSBs respond capably, the contracting officer can justify an SDVOSB set-aside under the rule of two — meaning your two-page response can remove most of America from the competition before it starts. Respond to two per month in your lanes. Standard skeleton:

  1. Company identity block (UEI, CAGE, NAICS, SDVOSB certification, contacts).
  2. Capability mapping — their stated need × your offers, point by point, in their vocabulary.
  3. Relevant experience — 2–3 past-performance snapshots with metrics.
  4. Socioeconomic statement — certified SDVOSB; respectfully note set-aside suitability and that capable SDVOSBs exist.
  5. Logistics — capacity, start availability, place of performance, teaming posture.
Done whenA reusable response template exists and the first two live responses are filed.

Subcontract and team before you prime

Make VA your beachhead agency

Cost $0 (+travel as justified)Time 2–4 hrs/wkDepends on 3.2

VA also holds Vets First sole-source authority — relationships built now mature into direct awards later.

Done whenVA OSDBU briefing held; two VA opportunities live in the pipeline tracker.

Most SDVOSB status losses are self-inflicted paperwork failures: lapsed SAM, missed recertification, or an investor document that quietly conditioned control. Nothing in growth is worth those mistakes.

Scams & pitfalls
Read once now, again when the phone rings

The predators and the potholes.

Scams aimed at new registrants

The pitchThe truth
"Your SAM registration expires — renew for $599"Renewal is free at sam.gov. These mills scrape new registrations and spoof urgency. Official mail comes from .gov.
"We'll get you SDVOSB-certified — $3,000"VetCert is free and takes about two hours of your time. No third party is required or advantaged.
"Guaranteed GSA Schedule fast-track"Nobody can guarantee a schedule award, and you may not even want MAS yet (see step 6.2).
"Buy our list of set-aside contracts"SAM.gov publishes every opportunity free, with better filters than the resellers.
"EIN filing service — $250"The IRS issues EINs free in 15 minutes (step 1.3).

Self-inflicted pitfalls (more expensive than the scams)

  1. Waiting for certification to start selling. Commercial revenue needs no certification; the model breaks even without a single federal dollar.
  2. A template operating agreement that surrenders control. The cheapest place to lose SDVOSB status is paragraph 14 of a $40 download (step 1.2 exists for a reason).
  3. One-agency, one-contract dependence. The concentration cap (≤40% of trailing revenue per client) applies to government clients too.
  4. Ignoring sources sought. Set-asides are created by responses (step 4.6) — bidders who only watch RFPs compete in everyone's pool.
  5. Letting SAM lapse mid-pursuit. The renewal alarm in step 6.1 is not optional.
  6. Pricing federal work off hope. The rate card (step 4.2) keeps your price defensible and your margin honest.
  7. Mixing personal and business money. Commingling wrecks bookkeeping, taxes, liability protection, and examiner confidence in control — all at once.

FAQ
Straight answers

Questions veterans actually ask.

Does a 0% disability rating qualify?

Yes. Any VA service-connected rating qualifies for SDVOSB status, including 0%. What matters is the service-connected determination in VA records, shown on your Benefit Summary Letter.

Can I keep a day job while I launch?

Carefully, and temporarily. SBA control rules expect the veteran to manage day-to-day operations and generally work full-time in the business during normal business hours; a concurrent full-time W-2 job invites a control finding against you. The clean path: build commercially while employed if you must, but have a credible transition story — and be fully devoted to AAS by the time you apply in Phase 3. Discuss specifics with your VBOC counselor before applying.

Can my spouse or a partner co-own the company?

Yes — if you keep at least 51% direct, unconditional ownership and full control, and the operating agreement gives no one veto rights over your decisions. Every ownership change gets re-screened against 13 CFR Part 128 before signing.

How long until the first federal dollar?

Realistically 6–18 months for meaningful awards — which is why this plan sells commercially from month one, pursues micro-purchases and subcontracts early, and treats primes as customers too. The certification itself is fast now (~12-day average); the relationships are the long lead item.

Do I need an office, employees, or a security clearance?

No, no, and almost certainly not for this work. Remote single-member SDVOSBs win services contracts routinely. Clearances become relevant only for specific classified environments — cross that bridge if a real opportunity requires it.

Should I take the SBA loan?

The base plan cash-flows without debt. The SBA Express line (no upfront guaranty fee for veteran-owned businesses) is worth opening as standby capacity for federal net-30/net-60 payment cycles — cheap insurance, not fuel.

What single mistake should I be most paranoid about?

Any document that conditions your ownership or control — investor terms, partner buy-sell clauses, even a well-meaning template. The status that opens this market lives or dies in paperwork you sign. When in doubt: attorney first, signature second.
