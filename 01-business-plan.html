Section 01 — Executive summary
01 · Executive summary

The operating partner for measurable AI execution.

Advanced Agentic Systems modernizes how work moves across teams, systems, decisions, and growth channels. We are not an AI readiness consultancy, a chatbot builder, or a dev shop. Every engagement starts with evidence and ends with an operating decision.

The problem

Organizations are buying AI faster than they can absorb it. Tools get licensed, pilots get demoed, and the work still moves by hand — status chased in meetings, decisions routed by memory, reports rebuilt in spreadsheets. The cost of that friction is real but invisible, because nobody has mapped the workflow or priced the waste. As AI-assisted development gets cheaper every quarter, the scarce capability is no longer building automation. It is knowing what to build, proving what it is worth, and owning adoption until the result is measured.

The answer

AAS sells a disciplined ladder, not a menu of automations. A Workshop builds trust and surfaces hidden demand. The Foundation audit — four weeks, fixed fee — maps how work actually moves, prices the friction in dollars, and ranks what to fix first. The Momentum pilot builds the first production workflow from that roadmap. The Operating Partner retainer keeps results compounding: monitoring, optimization, expansion, quarterly operating reviews. A parallel Agentic AI Agency line builds the brand, web, chat, voice, marketing, and CRM systems that growing companies run on.

Two engines, one company

Engine one is commercial. Service businesses and mid-market operators buy audits and growth systems now, generate cash in weeks, and build the proof library. Engine two is federal. As a service-disabled veteran-owned small business, AAS gains access to a market that set aside $28.6 billion for SDVOSB firms in FY2025 — and where the government missed its own 5% goal, leaving agencies under pressure to find certified, capable vendors. Commercial proof feeds federal credibility. Federal scale feeds enterprise value.

Workshop and Foundation: earn the right to build.

Workshop — build the funnel before you need it

The workshop is not the business; it creates qualified demand for Foundation. AAS teaches the market how to find friction and price it, builds executive relationships, and leaves each session with one workflow candidate and one named sponsor. Two industry outlines exist at launch — CPA firms and generic service businesses — with more presets added as the platform taxonomy grows.

  • Qualification checklist applied in every session: pain, sponsor, workflow scope, budget, urgency, data access.
  • Conversion target: one in three workshops yields a qualified audit conversation within 60 days (planning assumption, tracked from day one).

Foundation — the productized diagnostic

Foundation packages what clients cannot easily do themselves: objective workflow truth, a financial model of their own friction, and implementation priority. Four weeks, fixed $25K, senior-led. Every readout ends with a pre-briefed sponsor and a decision call within five business days.

Why now.

Work waits for people

Teams chase status, route decisions, and rebuild reports by hand. AI tools are licensed but sit outside the operating model. Pilots demo well and die in adoption.

After

Systems move the work

Three shifts converge in 2026

Federal demand — the SDVOSB advantage

A mandated market that just missed its own target.

Congress raised the government-wide SDVOSB prime contracting goal from 3% to 5% in the FY2024 NDAA. In FY2025 agencies awarded roughly $28.6 billion to SDVOSBs across some 52,000 contract actions — and still fell over $2 billion short of the goal. A miss is pressure: contracting officers and OSDBU offices are actively hunting for certified SDVOSBs they can award to, especially in capability areas where few exist. Agentic AI is exactly such an area.

The AI spending wave

The Department of Defense requested $13.4 billion for AI and autonomous systems in FY2026 — its first dedicated AI budget line — while federal AI contracts grew from 472 in 2022 to over 1,700 in 2026, and combined federal AI spending (procurement, R&D, infrastructure) crossed $100 billion. Critically for AAS, most of this is not exotic R&D: it is workflow automation, document processing, decision support, and adoption — the unglamorous middle where AAS operates.

Section 08 — Operations & delivery
08 · Operations & delivery

A connected operating model — applied to ourselves.

Delivery methodology

Discover → Design → Approve → Automate → Measure. The audit runs a seven-phase protocol inside the platform; pilots run scoped build sprints with a 30-day stabilization window; retainers run a monthly optimization cadence with quarterly operating reviews. People, process, systems, visibility — standardized across every engagement.

Quality rules that are also trust assets

Q·01

Loaded hourly cost, never billing rate. Leakage separated from labor waste in every report.

Q·02

Every friction instance carries a documented source: observed, sampled, self-report, or system log.

Q·03

Never assume 100% recovery. Conservative and optimistic scenarios shown side by side.

Q·04

AI never writes a numeric field. Published snapshots are immutable. Every number traces to a source.

Capacity & tooling

  • Capacity model: founder delivery capped at ~60% of working time; the rest is reserved for sales, content, and federal relationship-building. A vetted subcontractor bench absorbs build surges before the first hire.
  • Tool posture: tool-agnostic, client-stack-first. AAS owns its method and platform; it does not resell licenses or take referral kickbacks that bias recommendations.

Security & compliance posture

  • Client data minimization, encrypted storage, access on least privilege; signed DPAs where client data is touched.
  • Insurance: general liability, professional liability (E&O), and cyber liability in force before enterprise and federal work.
  • Federal trajectory: no CUI handling at launch; NIST 800-171 self-assessment and SPRS score prepared when DoD subcontracts appear; platform v3 GovCloud path keeps the long-term FedRAMP-aware option open.
