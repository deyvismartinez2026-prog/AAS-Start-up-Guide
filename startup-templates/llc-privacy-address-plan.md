# LLC Privacy + Address Plan

Purpose: reduce unnecessary public exposure while staying truthful and compliant. This is not legal advice; confirm with a Florida attorney/CPA/registered agent before filing.

## Privacy goal
- Keep home address off public-facing materials wherever legally possible.
- Use a registered agent for service of process.
- Use a commercial/digital office or CMRA mailbox for business mail handling.
- Use business email and business phone for public records and public-facing accounts.

## What can still become visible
- Florida business filings are public records.
- Sunbiz notes that Florida email addresses may be public records.
- Principal office address requires a street address; a P.O. Box is not acceptable for principal office address.
- Mailing address may be different from principal office and may support P.O. Box/CMRA depending on provider and form rules.
- Banks, IRS, SAM.gov, payment processors, and certification bodies may require non-public identity verification and beneficial owner/responsible party information.

## Filing rule of thumb
Do not file the LLC until the address plan is settled. It is easier to keep a private home address out at the beginning than to correct it later after it has already been indexed.
